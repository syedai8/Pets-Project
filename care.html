How to Take Care of Them!

Tips:

  1. Prepare a Safe, Quiet Space: Set up a small, secure room with food, water, a litter box, scratching post, toys, and a cozy bed. This gives the cat a calm place to decompress.
  2. Cat-proof Your Home: Remove toxic plants, secure cords, hide small objects, and ensure windows and balconies are safe.
  3. Stock Up on Essentials: Food (preferably what the cat is already eating), litter, bowls, a carrier, grooming tools, and a scratching surface.
  4. Give Them Time to Adjust: Cats may hide, avoid contact, or be shy at first. Let them come to you on their own terms.
  5. Keep a Routine: Offer meals, play, and quiet time at consistent hours. Stability helps them feel secure.
  6. Introduce Slowly: If you have other pets, introduce them gradually, scent swapping first, then visual meetings, then brief supervised encounters.
  7. Use Positive Reinforcement: Treats, gentle play, and soft interactions help build trust.
  8. Respect Their Boundaries: Learn their communication cues such as tail, ears, and posture say a lot.
  9. Provide Enrichment: Offer puzzle feeders, climbing spaces, window perches, and regular play to keep them mentally and physically stimulated.
  10. Schedule a Vet Visit: Even if the cat was recently checked, a vet introduction is helpful to establish care and update vaccines or microchip info.
  11. Maintain Proper Identification: Microchip information should be updated with your details and consider a breakaway collar with a tag.
  12. Keep Up With Grooming & Preventatives: Brushing, nail trimming, and flea/tick/parasite prevention keep your cat healthy and comfortable.
  13. Understand That Every Cat Is Different: Personalities range from outgoing to reserved so let them blossom at their own pace.
  14. Be Patient With Setbacks: Accidents or anxious behavior can happen during transitions but avoid punishment and focus on positive solutions.
